# Product

## Register

product

## Users

MuscleMap Fitness is for gym trainees who already have some structure to their training and want a faster way to choose muscles, inspect relevant exercises, start a workout, and keep local training history. Users are typically on a phone at the gym, between sets, or planning the next session before training.

## Product Purpose

The product turns muscle selection into a practical training workflow: pick a body area, find matching exercises, add movements to the current workout, follow recent plans, and review training history. Success means the home screen helps users resume or start the next useful action without duplicating the bottom navigation or turning training into random daily inspiration.

## Brand Personality

Playful, athletic, and precise. The interface should feel more like a polished fitness cockpit than a generic admin dashboard: vivid enough to be motivating, but still clear under gym lighting and fast to use one-handed.

## Anti-references

Avoid a redundant menu homepage that repeats every tab in the bottom navigation. Avoid generic SaaS cards, beige wellness minimalism, heavy marketing hero copy, stock photo fitness cliches, and decorative anatomy that does not map clearly to the interactive muscle targets.

## Design Principles

- Workflow first: every home element should lead to a real training action or recently relevant state.
- Muscle map as the product signature: the body visual should be accurate enough to orient the user and playful enough to feel memorable.
- Do not fight existing plans: the app should support planned training rather than suggest arbitrary "today's inspiration".
- Dense but readable on mobile: prioritize thumb-sized controls, strong hierarchy, and no horizontal overflow.
- Local trust: make stored plans, active workouts, history, and backups feel predictable and recoverable.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text and controls. Muscle selection cannot rely on color alone; labels, focus rings, and route state should identify the chosen area. Motion should be subtle and non-blocking, with no critical information hidden behind hover-only states on touch devices.
